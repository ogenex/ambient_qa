Repetition and errors

Repetitive tasks and error-prone processes are prime candidates for automation. Be honest — this is exactly what we need to hear.

1What task do you repeat most often that follows almost exactly the same steps each time?
Even small tasks count — if you do it daily, it adds up.
2Where do mistakes most commonly happen in your work, and what causes them?
Think about errors you've made or seen — missed steps, wrong data, miscommunication.
3When errors happen, what's the typical impact?
4Tasks you do differently from how you were originally taught? (optional)
This often reveals undocumented improvements that should be formalised.
Step 5

Knowledge and systems

We want to understand where knowledge is locked inside people's heads rather than documented — and where the tools we use create friction rather than remove it.

1What's the one thing that would be very hard for someone else to cover if you were absent?
What would fall over without you — because only you know how to do it?
2What do new team members or others in the business ask you most often?
Frequently-asked questions usually mean there's undocumented knowledge or unclear processes.
3Which systems or tools cause the most frustration in your day?
Name the tools and describe the pain — too many steps, poor visibility, duplicate entry, etc.
4How documented are your core processes?
